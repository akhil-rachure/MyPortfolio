Sentiment Analysis of Amazon Fine Food Reviews

Analyzing the polarity of Amazon fine food reviews through implementation of various Machine Learning Algorithms.

GitHub

Project Overview

The project aimed to analyze the polarity of Amazon fine food reviews, utilizing a dataset sourced from Kaggle. The primary objective was to determine the sentiment of reviews, categorizing them as either positive or negative.

Project Execution

Exploratory Data Analysis: During the EDA phase, duplicates were identified and removed, and missing values were appropriately handled. Key statistics of the dataset were summarized, shedding light on the distribution of ratings.

Preprocessing: Steps were taken to clean and preprocess the text data, including the removal of HTML tags, punctuation, and stopwords. Notably, Snowball Stemming was employed for stemming the word to common root.

Machine Learning: The machine learning process involved converting text to vectors using techniques such as Bag of Words, TF-IDF, Average Word2Vec, and TF-IDF Word2Vec. Various ML models were applied, accompanied by thorough hyperparameter tuning. Feature engineering was explored, incorporating factors like the length of reviews.

Results and Visualization: Outcomes of the machine learning models were analyzed, showcasing metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. Visualizations, created using Seaborn and Matplotlib, were presented to enhance understanding.
